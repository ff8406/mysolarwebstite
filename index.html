Solar System

The solar system consists of the sun, the eight planets and the satellites. Other than these, there are asteroids, comets, dust, minor planets, and gas. The Sun, Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars constitute the inner solar system, and the asteroid belt lies between the orbit of Mars and Jupiter. .

The Sun

The sun is a huge star present at the centre of the solar system, and all the eight planets revolve around the sun. It is a huge ball of fire and gases. It is formed of various types of gases like hydrogen, helium, oxygen, etc. The sun gives us heat and light for the environment and the growth of organisms.

Mercury

Living up to its name, Mercury is the fastest planet in the solar system, speeding along at about 29 miles per second and completing each orbit around the sun in just 88 Earth days. Mercury is also the smallest planet in the solar system, measuring just 3,032 miles wide at its equator.
